Death Stranding

Surrounded by death, Sam Bridges must confront a world completely transformed by the Death Stranding (an apocalyptic event). Carrying the last hopes of a future, Sam embarks on a journey to rebuild the annihilated world, one step at a time. It is a game about rebuilding the world after a mysterious cataclysm. About trying to create connections in an empty, divided, and inherently hostile environment. About helping and relying on people you have never seen or will see, and how sometimes this help can be rewarded in unexpected ways.

Gameplay

The concept of the "strand game" introduced by Hideo Kojima emphasizes the importance of connectivity between players. Structures and resources left by other players can be shared, facilitating the protagonist's traversal. The use of advanced equipment, such as the backpack with stabilizers, motorized exoskeleton, and "baby pod" devices, adds complexity and innovation to the gameplay. Additionally, environmental challenges and supernatural creatures known as BTs maintain constant tension, contributing to an engaging and unique gaming experience.

Hideo Kojima

Hideo Kojima is a renowned Japanese game designer, known for his avant-garde vision and intricate narratives. As the founder of Kojima Productions, he is acclaimed for his work on the Metal Gear series and, more recently, the innovative Death Stranding. Kojima is recognized not only for his contributions to the video game industry but also for his ventures into cinema and the creation of interactive experiences that challenge traditional conventions. His authorial approach and ability to explore new narrative territories have solidified his position as an iconic figure in the world of electronic games.
